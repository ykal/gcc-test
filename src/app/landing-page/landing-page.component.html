About GCC

Girls Can Code (GCC) is a project initiated by the U.S. Embassy, aimed to equip and inspire female high school students with computer coding and leadership skills. Since its launch in October 2015, GCC has trained more than 350 girls from different public schools in Addis Ababa, Bahar Dar, Jimma, Dire Dawa and Mekelle. Currently, the Embassy is partnering with iCog Labs-ACC to implement the GCC Next Generation camps in 10 public universities and 4 American Corners to inspire more girls in different locations.

What we offer

GCC Toolkit

The GCC toolkit includes training materials and other necessary items to help trainers promote, deliver and monitor the camps.

TRAINING OF TRAINERS

All GCC Next Gen trainers receive an intensive Training of Trainers mainly focusing on the methodology of the content.

GCC NEXT GEN CAMP

We work with partnering institutions to host camps for girls who are enrolling in public school to give coding and life skill training for free.

ALUMNI ENGAGEMENT and NETWORKING

Facilitate GCC alumni (trainers and students) engagement with each other, with professionals in the industry and institutions with constant mentorship and follow up.

CAT

Students required to take and pass the CAT (CONTINUOUS ASSESSMENT TEST) to continue to the next level. And also awarded with certifications.
